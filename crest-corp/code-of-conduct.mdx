Crest is a fully distributed team across multiple time zones. The behaviors on this page exist so that distributed work stays trustworthy, decisions stay auditable, and confidential information stays inside the company.

## Meetings and standups

- **Camera on for every Zoom meeting. No exceptions.** This includes one-on-ones, standups, design reviews, and incident calls. If your camera is broken, fix it before your next meeting; do not join with video off.
- **Daily standup at 10 AM ET on Zoom.** Attendance is mandatory for engineers and project leads. If you cannot attend, post your update in the project Slack channel before standup starts.
- **Be on time.** A 10 AM standup means you are on the call by 09:59. Repeated lateness affects the daily review.
- **Use your real name.** Zoom display name should match the name on your Deel profile and your `@creststaff.com` account. No nicknames, no "iPhone."

## Decisions in public

- **Decisions are made in public Slack channels.** If a decision happens in a DM, in a 1:1, or in a private group, the person who made it is responsible for posting a summary to the relevant project channel within the same business day.
- **No "shadow decisions" via DM.** If a teammate tries to get you to commit to scope, deadline, or architecture in a DM, redirect to the project channel. This is not optional; it is how distributed teams stay coordinated.
- **Document architectural decisions.** Anything that affects more than one repo or more than one team should land in a Linear document or a docs page (this site), not in a Slack thread that scrolls away.

## Recording and AI assistants

<Warning>
**Do not record meetings with personal note-takers, AI assistants, or third-party transcription tools.** This includes Otter, Fireflies, Read, Grain, personal Zoom AI Companion, and any other tool not explicitly sanctioned. Crest operates its own meeting recording and transcription pipeline; bringing your own creates an unmanaged data leak.
</Warning>

- The only sanctioned meeting recording is the official Crest Zoom recording, captured by the host or by Crest automation.
- If you need a transcript for personal recall, use the official transcript that Crest generates from the sanctioned recording; ask Bryant for access.
- Joining a Crest meeting with a third-party note-taker bot is a security event. The bot will be removed and the incident logged.

## Confidentiality

The confidentiality and IP rules are covered in the Engineering Rules. Two specific items worth highlighting on this page:

- **Marcia substrate is trade secret.** Do not name underlying models, providers, or fine-tune sources in any external material. This applies to LinkedIn, conference talks, podcasts, blog posts, and DMs to people outside Crest. See [Engineering Rule 9](/onboarding/engineering-rules).
- **All work product belongs to Crest.** Code, designs, research notes, and meeting recordings produced under your Crest contract are Crest's. See [Engineering Rule 10](/onboarding/engineering-rules).

## Communication norms

- **Slack is asynchronous by default.** Do not expect a reply within minutes outside business hours unless something is on fire.
- **Use threads.** Channel-top messages should be a thread starter, not a 12-message stream-of-consciousness. Threads keep channels readable.
- **Tag your project lead, not the channel,** unless the message genuinely needs everyone. `@channel` and `@here` are reserved for incidents.
- **Be specific about what you need.** "Can someone help with the auth service?" is less useful than "Can someone with NestJS auth experience pair with me on `crestpetsystem` PR #4651 for 20 minutes?"

## Escalation

If something feels wrong (a teammate's behavior, a request you cannot reconcile with policy, a decision you think is unsafe), escalate. The order is:

1. Talk to the person directly if you can do it respectfully
2. If not, talk to your project lead
3. If your project lead is the issue, DM Bryant
4. If it is sensitive, DM Michael

There is no penalty for raising a concern. There is a penalty for staying silent and watching something break.
