<Warning>
**If Hubstaff is not running, you are not on the clock.** Time not tracked in Hubstaff will not be paid. There is no retroactive entry process for forgotten sessions.
</Warning>

Hubstaff is how we measure billable work, attribute time to the right project, and audit invoices. It is part of every Crest contract. Treat it as load-bearing infrastructure, not a suggestion.

## The rules

1. **Hubstaff must be running during all paid work hours.** Start it when you start, stop it when you stop. No exceptions.
2. **Use the desktop app, not the web interface.** The web interface does not capture activity (keystrokes, mouse, screenshots) and will be flagged in the daily review.
3. **Assign time to the correct project and task.** The default project for engineers is **CrestPetSystem**. Your project lead will tell you the right task. Wrong project means wrong invoice means a delayed payment.
4. **Daily activity tracking is part of the contract.** Keystrokes, mouse activity, and periodic screenshots are captured. This is disclosed in your contract. If you are uncomfortable with this, raise it with Bryant before starting work.
5. **Stop Hubstaff for unpaid breaks.** Lunch, errands, personal calls. Crest does not pay for break time. Run Hubstaff back up when you return.
6. **Stop Hubstaff at the end of your day.** Do not leave it running overnight. Idle time will be detected and removed by the daily review, but leaving it running creates noise that takes time to clean up.

## Daily review

Every paid hour goes through an automated review the next morning. The review:

- Confirms the time was assigned to a real project and task
- Removes idle time (no keystrokes, no mouse activity for >10 minutes)
- Flags screenshots that look unrelated to assigned work
- Compares total hours to your contract terms

If anything looks off, you will get a Slack message asking for clarification before the time is submitted to invoicing. Respond same day; unresponded clarifications default to "remove."

## Time off and vacation

If you have planned time off, give Bryant or your project lead at least 5 business days of notice for short absences (1 to 3 days) and at least 2 weeks for longer absences. Do not run Hubstaff during time off, even partially.

Sick days and short personal interruptions do not need pre-approval. Just stop Hubstaff and let your project lead know in your daily standup.

## Common pitfalls

- **Forgetting to start Hubstaff** when you sit down to work. Set a calendar reminder for the first week.
- **Wrong project assignment.** If you have access to multiple Crest projects, double-check the project picker every morning.
- **Web interface instead of desktop.** The web interface looks like it is tracking but it is not capturing activity. The daily review will flag this and the time will be removed.
- **Running Hubstaff while in a meeting that is not Crest work.** Personal calls, doctor appointments, errands — stop Hubstaff first.
- **Leaving Hubstaff running while AFK for hours.** Idle time will be removed automatically, but it indicates you forgot. Practice stopping at the end of every block of work.
