The **Talent Pool** is a Linear team that coordinates scoped, time-bound help across Crest. When a dev, lead, or PM needs extra hands on a deliverable, they submit a project here. Michael reviews, approves, and allocates talent; the talent delivers the scoped work; they roll off to the next request.

This replaces ad-hoc "can someone pick this up?" pings in Slack.

<Note>
Linear team: **Talent Pool (`TP`)**. Open Linear, switch workspace to Crest USA, pick the team from the sidebar. If you cannot see it, DM Michael to be added.
</Note>

## Timeline — plan ahead

This is **not** a "next week" service. The end-to-end timeline is 6 to 8 weeks from submission to delivered:

| Phase | Duration | What's happening |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Submission → Approval | **1 to 2 weeks** | Michael reviews scope, validates estimate, confirms fit. Requests missing scope or sub-issues get bounced back. |
| Approval → Staffed | **~3 weeks** | Vendor outreach, Deel contracting, background check, tool/access provisioning, context ramp. |
| Staffed → Delivered | **2+ weeks** | One full cycle minimum of actual work. |
| Delivered → Rolled Off | 1 to 2 days | Acceptance by requester + rolloff retro by Michael. |

**If your deadline is less than 6 weeks from today, Talent Pool is the wrong tool.** Escalate to your own team's lead instead.

## Minimum bar for a request

Every request must meet these at intake. They are enforced at approval — requests that don't clear the bar are rejected with comments explaining what to fix.

- **At least 2 weeks (1 cycle, ~80 hours) of scoped work.** Anything smaller should stay on the requesting team.
- **At least 4 sub-issues** inside the project covering the work breakdown. If you can't find 4 discrete pieces of work, the scope isn't decomposed enough yet.
- **A concrete deliverable** — specific artifacts, not vague outcomes. "Stripe webhook coverage for 4 event types with integration tests" is concrete; "improve billing" is not.
- **A hard deadline** (or "none" with a target start date). Deadlines under 6 weeks will be rejected — see the timeline above.

## When to use the Talent Pool

Use it when all of these are true:

- The work is **scoped** with a clear deliverable
- The work is **at least 2 weeks** of one person's time
- Your **deadline is 6+ weeks out** (or there is no hard deadline)
- You can **break it into 4+ sub-issues** at intake
- You need **someone other than yourself** to do it
- It is **not** product roadmap work your team already owns

Do NOT use it for:

- Deadlines under 6 weeks from today — wrong tool, escalate to your lead
- Work shorter than 2 weeks — stay on your own team
- Scope you can't decompose into 4+ sub-issues — scope it more first
- Product roadmap work on your team (stay on your team's board)
- Infra incidents or outages (use the Infrastructure team)
- Access requests, onboarding, offboarding, IAM changes (use CrestCorp team)
- General support questions (use the Support team)

## The lifecycle, at a glance

<Steps>
  <Step title="Requester submits">
    Create a Linear project on the Talent Pool team with scope, estimate, deadline, **and at least 4 sub-issues**. Status: `Requested`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Triage + approval (1-2 weeks)">
    Michael reviews scope, validates estimate, confirms minimum bar is met. If approved, status moves to `Triaged`. If not, you'll get comments asking for scope, more sub-issues, or a bump back to your own team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Allocation (3 weeks)">
    Michael sources talent from internal bench, external vendors, or Deel-contracted talent. Vendor outreach, contracting, background checks, and access provisioning all happen in this window. Status moves to `Staffed` with a `Talent Source` label applied.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work happens (2+ weeks)">
    Talent moves the project to `In Progress`. Sub-issues cover the task breakdown. Requester stays reachable for clarifications.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deliverables go to review">
    Talent moves to `In Review` when PRs or artifacts are ready. Requester reviews and accepts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delivered and rolled off">
    On acceptance, status moves to `Delivered`. Michael closes out with a rolloff retrospective (hours reconciliation, learnings) and moves to `Rolled Off`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to submit a request (requesters)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check the timeline first">
    Work out: is your deadline 6+ weeks away? Is the work 2+ weeks? Can you break it into 4+ sub-issues? If any answer is no, Talent Pool is not the right tool.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Linear and switch to Talent Pool">
    Sidebar → workspace: Crest USA → team: **Talent Pool**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new project">
    Click **+ New Project**. Name format: `<your team> — <short scope phrase>`. Example: `CrestPet — Stripe webhooks coverage pass` or `Infra — Tailscale ACL refactor`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the intake template">
    Paste the template below into the project description and fill every section. Incomplete requests get bounced back at approval.

    ```markdown
    ## Scope
    <One paragraph: what needs to be built, fixed, or reviewed.>

    ## Deliverable
    <What "done" looks like. Specific artifacts, not vague outcomes.>

    ## Constraints / Links
    - Repo: <e.g., crestpetsystem>
    - Related Linear: <CPE-123, INF-456>
    - Docs / specs: <Figma, Notion, design doc>
    - Who to ask for clarification: <requester name + Slack handle>

    ## Estimate
    - Skill: <one of: fullstack, frontend-dev, backend-dev, devops-skill, qa, data>
    - Hours: <raw estimate, minimum ~80>
    - Size label: <M (1 cycle / ~80h), L (2 cycles / ~160h), or XL (3+ cycles)>
    - Hard deadline (EST): <YYYY-MM-DD — must be 6+ weeks out, or "none">
    - Target start: <YYYY-MM-DD — allow 4-5 weeks from submission>

    ## Sub-issue Breakdown (minimum 4)
    Create at least 4 sub-issues inside this project covering the work. List them here:
    1. <Sub-issue 1 title>
    2. <Sub-issue 2 title>
    3. <Sub-issue 3 title>
    4. <Sub-issue 4 title>

    ## Acceptance Criteria
    - [ ] <Checklist item 1>
    - [ ] <Checklist item 2>
    - [ ] Code review passed
    - [ ] Requester signs off

    ## Non-goals (scope guard)
    <What is explicitly OUT of scope — prevents drift.>
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the sub-issues in Linear">
    Inside the new project, create at least 4 Linear issues covering the breakdown. Without these, the request will not be approved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set status to Requested">
    New projects default to `Backlog` — change to `Requested`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply labels">
    Required: `resource-request` + one **Skill** label + one **Size** label (M, L, or XL). Michael adds the **Talent Source** label after allocation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally dual-home the project">
    If you want the project to also appear on your own team's board, add your team under **Project settings → Teams**. The project shows in both places without duplication.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
After submission, expect **1 to 2 weeks** for approval and another **~3 weeks** before an allocated resource starts work. Build that into your project plan.
</Note>

## How to fulfill a request (talent)

<Steps>
  <Step title="You get assigned">
    Michael adds you to the project and assigns you to the root issues when the status moves to `Staffed`. You will get a Linear notification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read before you start">
    Read the scope, deliverable, sub-issues, and acceptance criteria in full. Ask clarifying questions in a project comment tagging the requester. Do not start implementation until ambiguity is resolved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move to In Progress">
    Change the project status when you actually start. This signals to the requester that work has begun.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work the sub-issues">
    Assign each of the 4+ sub-issues to yourself. Move them through status as you complete them. Create additional sub-issues if the breakdown surfaces more work during execution.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for review">
    When PRs or deliverables are ready, move the project to `In Review` and tag the requester in a comment with links to the artifacts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for acceptance, then roll off">
    Requester accepts and moves to `Delivered`. Michael runs the rolloff retro and moves to `Rolled Off`. You are now available for the next request.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Labels reference

Every resource request is tagged across four dimensions. Requesters fill in the first three, Michael adds the fourth after allocation.

| Group              | Options                                                                                   | Who applies          |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| `resource-request` | (single label, always applied)                                                             | Requester            |
| **Skill**          | `fullstack`, `frontend-dev`, `backend-dev`, `devops-skill`, `qa`, `data`                   | Requester            |
| **Size**           | `M (1 cycle / ~80h)`, `L (2 cycles / ~160h)`, `XL (3+ cycles)`                              | Requester            |
| **Talent Source**  | `internal`, `vendor`, `deel`, `new-hire`                                                   | Triage (post-staff)  |

**Talent Source semantics:**

- `internal` — existing Crest staff with bench capacity
- `vendor` — sourced through an external staffing vendor or agency
- `deel` — contract and payment run through [Deel](/crest-corp/deel-setup) (Crest's primary contracting rail for external talent)
- `new-hire` — net-new person brought in specifically for this engagement

`vendor` and `deel` frequently stack on the same request — someone sourced through an agency whose contract also runs on Deel.

## Rules of the road

- **2-week work minimum.** One cycle or more. Anything smaller stays on your own team.
- **4-subissue minimum at intake.** No 4 sub-issues, no approval.
- **6-week deadline floor.** If you need it in less than 6 weeks, escalate to your lead, don't submit here.
- **One active project per talent.** They stay focused; requesters get dedicated attention. Exceptions require Michael's sign-off.
- **Two open projects per requester.** Prevents one team from hoarding all the bench capacity. A third submission has to wait until one closes.
- **`Blocked` does not count as active work.** If your project is blocked on an external dependency, the allocated talent can pick up a second one while the first sits.
- **Scope drift spawns a new ticket.** If something new surfaces mid-project that is not in scope, it becomes a new issue on the appropriate team (not an expansion of your Talent Pool project).

## Rolloff retrospective

Every completed project gets a short retro comment from Michael when it moves to `Rolled Off`. It covers:

- Estimated hours vs actual hours (variance percentage)
- Whether scope held, expanded, or shrank
- Review quality (merged first try, or rounds of rework)
- Who did the work and whether Crest would reuse them
- One paragraph of learnings for future estimation

Three requests in a row from the same requester with more than ±50% variance triggers a conversation about estimate calibration.

## FAQ

**Why is the approval step 1 to 2 weeks?**
Scope review + estimate validation is real work. Most requests need at least one round of back-and-forth to tighten scope, confirm the sub-issue breakdown, or re-scope once Michael asks what "done" looks like. Rushing approval produces under-scoped projects that blow up mid-engagement.

**Why is there a 3-week lead time after approval?**
Sourcing, vendor outreach, Deel contracting, background check (if needed), access provisioning (GitHub, Tailscale, AWS, repos), and 2-3 days of context ramp so the talent can actually hit the ground running. You cannot compress this to days without ending up with someone who spends their first week confused.

**Why the 4-subtask minimum?**
It is the cheapest filter for under-scoped requests. If a requester can't identify 4 discrete pieces of work, they don't understand the scope well enough for someone else to execute it. Rejecting under-scoped requests at intake saves 2-4 weeks of rework later.

**Can I request a specific person?**
You can ask in the project description, but Michael may allocate differently based on capacity and fit. If the ask requires specific domain knowledge, flag that explicitly.

**What if I have unused hours in someone's Deel contract?**
Mention it in the request. Matching existing contracted capacity to scoped requests is part of how Michael triages — and it may cut the 3-week lead time if the person already has access.

**My request is bigger than 3 cycles. Should I still submit?**
Yes, but expect Michael to split it into multiple Talent Pool projects tied to milestones, or push it back to your team's roadmap. `XL (3+ cycles)` is a signal to reconsider, not a hard block.

**My deadline is in 4 weeks. Can you rush it?**
No. The 6-week floor is not a soft guideline — it reflects how long vendor sourcing and onboarding actually take. If your deadline is 4 weeks out, escalate to your own team's lead, not Talent Pool.

**Can external contractors watch the Talent Pool board?**
Yes. Contractors are added to the team as Linear guests or members depending on their engagement. Confidential scope goes in a private project; the default is open so capacity visibility stays shared.

**What about confidentiality?**
Default projects are team-visible. If a request touches legal, security, or M&A, mark the project private and restrict the member list. Michael can help set that up.
