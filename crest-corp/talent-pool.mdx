The **Talent Pool** is Crest's quarterly contractor engagement pool. When a lead or PM needs extra hands for a quarter-length initiative, they submit a project here. Michael reviews and approves, a resource is sourced, and the resource stays allocated to the project for approximately three months before rolling off to the next engagement.

This is not a short-term helpdesk. It is a way to bring one dedicated body onto your scope for a full quarter.

<Note>
Linear team: **Talent Pool (`TP`)**. Open Linear, switch workspace to Crest USA, pick the team from the sidebar. If you cannot see it, DM Michael to be added.
</Note>

## The shape of a Talent Pool engagement

- **Engagement length: ~3 months.** The allocated resource stays with your project for roughly one quarter.
- **Scoped work at intake: at least 1 month (ideally 2 months)** of concretely broken-down work ready to execute on day one.
- **The remaining 1-2 months emerges during the engagement** as work is delivered and new scope surfaces. Michael and the requester partner on keeping the pipeline of work filled.
- **If you can't identify at least 1 month of scoped work up front, do not submit** — a 3-month engagement starved of scope costs more than it returns.

## Timeline — plan ahead

This is **not** a near-term service. End-to-end timeline is approximately **4 to 5 months** from submission to delivered:

| Phase | Duration | What's happening |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Submission → Approval | **1 to 2 weeks** | Michael reviews scope, validates that 1-2 months of work is concretely ready, confirms fit. Requests under-scoped at intake get bounced back. |
| Approval → Staffed | **~3 weeks** | Vendor outreach, Deel contracting, background check, tool/access provisioning, context ramp. |
| Staffed → Delivered | **~3 months** | Full quarter of work. First 1-2 months executes the intake-scoped plan; remaining time covers scope that emerges during execution. |
| Delivered → Rolled Off | 1 to 2 days | Acceptance by requester + rolloff retrospective by Michael. |

**If your deadline is less than 4 months from today, Talent Pool is the wrong tool.** Escalate to your own team's lead instead.

## Minimum bar for a request

Every request must meet these at intake. They are enforced at approval — requests that don't clear the bar are rejected with comments explaining what to fix.

- **At least 1 month (ideally 2 months) of concretely scoped work** ready to start on day one. Four weeks of "we'll figure it out" does not count.
- **At least 4 sub-issues** inside the project covering the intake-scoped work. If you can't find 4 discrete pieces of work, the scope isn't decomposed enough yet.
- **A concrete deliverable** — specific artifacts, not vague outcomes. "Stripe webhook coverage for 4 event types with integration tests + billing dashboard v2 + 3 stale-bug fixes" is concrete; "improve billing" is not.
- **A hard deadline** (or "none" with a target start date). Deadlines under 4 months will be rejected — see the timeline above.
- **A plan for keeping the resource busy for 3 months.** If you only have 1 month of work and no plausible next month, do not submit.

## When to use the Talent Pool

Use it when all of these are true:

- You have a **quarter-length initiative** that needs a dedicated body
- You have **at least 1 month of scoped work** ready on day one (ideally 2)
- You have a **plausible pipeline** for the remaining 1-2 months
- Your **deadline is 4+ months out** (or there is no hard deadline)
- You can **break the first month into 4+ sub-issues** at intake
- It is **not** product roadmap work your team already owns

Do NOT use it for:

- Deadlines under 4 months from today — wrong tool, escalate to your lead
- Short-term help (under 1 month) — stay on your own team
- Scope where you only have weeks of work lined up — not enough to justify a quarter engagement
- Work you can't decompose into 4+ sub-issues — scope it more first
- Product roadmap work your team already owns (stay on your team's board)
- Infra incidents or outages (use the Infrastructure team)
- Access requests, onboarding, offboarding, IAM changes (use CrestCorp team)
- General support questions (use the Support team)

## The lifecycle, at a glance

<Steps>
  <Step title="Requester submits">
    Create a Linear project on the Talent Pool team with scope, 1-2 months of concretely scoped work, deadline, and at least 4 sub-issues. Status: `Requested`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approval (1-2 weeks)">
    Michael reviews scope, validates the 1-month intake floor, confirms sub-issue breakdown, and checks that the requester has a plausible pipeline for the full quarter. If approved, status moves to `Triaged`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Allocation (~3 weeks)">
    Michael sources talent from internal bench, external vendors, or Deel-contracted talent. Vendor outreach, contracting, background checks, and access provisioning all happen in this window. Status moves to `Staffed` with a `Talent Source` label applied.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Engagement (~3 months)">
    Talent works the sub-issues, then partners with the requester to scope remaining weeks as work progresses. Talent moves the project to `In Progress` on start.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deliverables go to review">
    At the end of the engagement, talent moves to `In Review` with PRs or artifacts. Requester reviews and accepts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delivered and rolled off">
    On acceptance, status moves to `Delivered`. Michael closes out with a rolloff retrospective (hours reconciliation, learnings, would-reuse decision) and moves to `Rolled Off`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to submit a request (requesters)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check the bar first">
    Work out: deadline 4+ months away? At least 1 month of scoped work ready? Plausible pipeline for 3 months total? Decomposable into 4+ sub-issues? If any answer is no, Talent Pool is not the right tool.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Linear and switch to Talent Pool">
    Sidebar → workspace: Crest USA → team: **Talent Pool**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new project">
    Click **+ New Project**. Name format: `<your team> — <short quarter-scope phrase>`. Example: `CrestPet — Q3 Stripe billing overhaul` or `Infra — Q3 Tailscale hardening sprint`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the intake template">
    Paste the template below into the project description and fill every section. Under-scoped requests get bounced back at approval.

    ```markdown
    ## Scope (quarter-level)
    <One paragraph: the quarter-length initiative that needs a dedicated body.>

    ## Deliverable
    <What "done" looks like after ~3 months. Specific artifacts, not vague outcomes.>

    ## Scoped work at intake (month 1 — and ideally month 2)
    <Bullet list of concrete work ready to start on day one. At minimum 1 month; ideally 2. This is what gets broken into sub-issues below.>

    ## Pipeline for remaining time
    <How you will keep the resource busy for the full ~3 months. Can reference followup initiatives, known bug backlogs, discovery spikes, etc.>

    ## Constraints / Links
    - Repo: <e.g., crestpetsystem>
    - Related Linear: <CPE-123, INF-456>
    - Docs / specs: <Figma, Notion, design doc>
    - Who to ask for clarification: <requester name + Slack handle>

    ## Estimate
    - Skill: <one of: fullstack, frontend-dev, backend-dev, devops-skill, qa, data>
    - Hours: <~480 for a standard quarter>
    - Size label: <M (1 month), L (2 months), XL (3 months — standard), or XXL (3+ months)>
    - Hard deadline (EST): <YYYY-MM-DD — must be 4+ months out, or "none">
    - Target start: <YYYY-MM-DD — allow 4-5 weeks from submission>

    ## Sub-issue Breakdown (minimum 4, covering month 1-2 scoped work)
    1. <Sub-issue 1 title>
    2. <Sub-issue 2 title>
    3. <Sub-issue 3 title>
    4. <Sub-issue 4 title>

    ## Acceptance Criteria
    - [ ] <Checklist item 1>
    - [ ] <Checklist item 2>
    - [ ] Code review passed
    - [ ] Requester signs off

    ## Non-goals (scope guard)
    <What is explicitly OUT of scope — prevents drift.>
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the sub-issues in Linear">
    Inside the new project, create at least 4 Linear issues covering the month 1-2 breakdown. Without these, the request will not be approved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set status to Requested">
    New projects default to `Backlog` — change to `Requested`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply labels">
    Required: `resource-request` + one **Skill** label + one **Size** label (M, L, XL, or XXL). Michael adds the **Talent Source** label after allocation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally dual-home the project">
    If you want the project to also appear on your own team's board, add your team under **Project settings → Teams**. The project shows in both places without duplication.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
After submission, expect **1 to 2 weeks** for approval and another **~3 weeks** before an allocated resource starts work. The resource stays on your project for approximately **3 months** after that. Total end-to-end: ~4-5 months.
</Note>

## How to fulfill a request (talent)

<Steps>
  <Step title="You get assigned">
    Michael adds you to the project and assigns you the root issues when the status moves to `Staffed`. You will get a Linear notification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read before you start">
    Read the full scope, 1-2 month breakdown, pipeline for remaining time, deliverable, and acceptance criteria. Ask clarifying questions in a project comment tagging the requester. Do not start implementation until ambiguity is resolved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move to In Progress">
    Change the project status when you actually start. This signals to the requester that work has begun.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work the month 1-2 sub-issues">
    Assign each of the intake sub-issues to yourself. Move them through status as you complete them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Partner with the requester on emerging scope">
    As month 1-2 work lands, partner with the requester to scope the remaining weeks. Create new sub-issues for emergent work. If scope dries up, flag it to Michael early.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for review">
    At the end of the engagement, move the project to `In Review` and tag the requester with links to all deliverables.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for acceptance, then roll off">
    Requester accepts and moves to `Delivered`. Michael runs the rolloff retro and moves to `Rolled Off`. You are now available for the next engagement.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Labels reference

Every resource request is tagged across four dimensions. Requesters fill in the first three, Michael adds the fourth after allocation.

| Group              | Options                                                                                                    | Who applies          |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| `resource-request` | (single label, always applied)                                                                              | Requester            |
| **Skill**          | `fullstack`, `frontend-dev`, `backend-dev`, `devops-skill`, `qa`, `data`                                    | Requester            |
| **Size**           | `M (1 month / ~160h)`, `L (2 months / ~320h)`, `XL (3 months / ~480h)` — standard, `XXL (3+ months)`        | Requester            |
| **Talent Source**  | `internal`, `vendor`, `deel`, `new-hire`                                                                    | Triage (post-staff)  |

**The default size is `XL (3 months)`.** Most engagements land there. `M` and `L` are for partial-quarter engagements; `XXL` is for extended quarters that probably should have been split into separate engagements.

**Talent Source semantics:**

- `internal` — existing Crest staff with bench capacity
- `vendor` — sourced through an external staffing vendor or agency
- `deel` — contract and payment run through [Deel](/crest-corp/deel-setup) (Crest's primary contracting rail for external talent)
- `new-hire` — net-new person brought in specifically for this engagement

`vendor` and `deel` frequently stack on the same request — someone sourced through an agency whose contract also runs on Deel.

## Rules of the road

- **Standard engagement = 3 months (one quarter).** Partial-quarter engagements exist but need explicit justification at intake.
- **1-month minimum scoped work at intake, 2 months ideal.** Less than that, stay on your own team.
- **4-subissue minimum at intake.** No 4 sub-issues, no approval.
- **4-month deadline floor.** If you need it in less than 4 months, escalate to your lead, don't submit here.
- **Plan for the full quarter.** Requesters must have a plausible pipeline for the resource's 3 months, not just the first month.
- **One active engagement per resource.** They stay focused; requesters get dedicated attention. Exceptions require Michael's sign-off.
- **One open engagement per requester.** Prevents any single team from capturing multiple quarter-length allocations. A second request has to wait until the first rolls off.
- **`Blocked` does not count as active work.** If your project is blocked on an external dependency, the allocated resource may be temporarily shifted to another project with your lead's sign-off.
- **Scope drift spawns a new ticket, not an extension.** If something surfaces mid-engagement that is outside the quarter scope, it becomes a new issue on the appropriate team (not growth of the Talent Pool engagement).

## Rolloff retrospective

Every completed engagement gets a short retro comment from Michael when it moves to `Rolled Off`. It covers:

- Estimated hours vs actual hours (variance percentage)
- Whether scope held, expanded, or shrank
- **Concretely-scoped-at-intake hours vs. discovered-during-engagement hours** — so we can spot requesters who submit thin and expand later
- Review quality (merged first try, or rounds of rework)
- Who did the work and whether Crest would reuse them
- One paragraph of learnings for future engagements

Requesters with three engagements in a row where discovered-during-engagement hours exceed 50% of total hours will be asked to change their intake approach, or lose priority for future Talent Pool requests.

## FAQ

**Why is the standard engagement 3 months?**
Because sourcing, contracting, background checks, access provisioning, and context ramp cost about 3-5 weeks of external overhead. An engagement shorter than a quarter has ramp-up costs that are too high relative to delivered work. The quarter-length floor ensures the engagement is worth the sourcing cost.

**Why is the approval step 1 to 2 weeks?**
Scope review and pipeline validation is real work. Most requests need at least one round of back-and-forth to tighten scope, confirm the sub-issue breakdown, or verify the requester has 3 months of work ready, not just one. Rushing approval produces under-scoped engagements that stall mid-quarter.

**Why is there a 3-week lead time after approval?**
Sourcing, vendor outreach, Deel contracting, background check (if needed), access provisioning (GitHub, Tailscale, AWS, repos), and 2-3 days of context ramp so the talent can actually hit the ground running. You cannot compress this to days.

**Why the 1-month scoped-at-intake floor?**
Because requesters who haven't thought through month 1 rarely have a plan for months 2-3 either. If you can't describe a month of work, you don't understand the scope well enough to hand it off for a quarter.

**Why the 4-subtask minimum?**
It is the cheapest filter for under-scoped requests. If a requester can't identify 4 discrete pieces of work in month 1, they don't understand the scope well enough for someone else to execute it.

**Can I request a partial-quarter engagement (M or L size)?**
Yes, but expect Michael to push back at approval. Partial quarters have the same sourcing overhead as full quarters, so the justification needs to be strong (e.g., "this is explicitly a 1-month feature push with a hard deadline I can't extend"). Default size is XL (3 months).

**Can I request a specific person?**
You can ask in the project description, but Michael may allocate differently based on capacity and fit.

**What if I have unused hours in someone's Deel contract?**
Mention it in the request. Matching existing contracted capacity to scoped requests is part of how Michael triages, and it may cut the 3-week lead time if the person already has access.

**My deadline is in 3 months. Can you rush it?**
No. The 4-month floor is not a soft guideline — it reflects how long sourcing, onboarding, AND the engagement itself take. If your deadline is 3 months out, Talent Pool cannot help; escalate to your own team's lead.

**My engagement is running out of scope mid-quarter. What do I do?**
Tell Michael immediately. Options: reassign the resource to another approved request, roll them off early, or (least preferred) have the requester scope a month of new work. Do not try to stretch thin work to fill the remaining time.

**Can external contractors watch the Talent Pool board?**
Yes. Contractors are added as Linear guests or members depending on their engagement. Confidential scope goes in a private project.

**What about confidentiality?**
Default projects are team-visible. If a request touches legal, security, or M&A, mark the project private and restrict the member list.
