Deel is how Crest pays you, collects your tax forms, and runs your background check. A complete Deel profile is a prerequisite for every other access we provision. Plan to finish it on your first day.

<Note>
Crest's Deel organization ID is `396225`. You will be added by Bryant or Karen and receive an email invitation directly from Deel. If you have not received the invitation by end of day one, DM Bryant.
</Note>

## What you will need

Have these ready before you start. Gathering them mid-flow is the most common reason profile setup spans multiple days.

- **Government-issued photo ID** (passport preferred; national ID or driver's license accepted depending on country)
- **Proof of address** dated within the last 90 days (utility bill, bank statement, lease, government letter)
- **Tax identification document** for your country (US: W-9 or W-8BEN; other countries: equivalent tax form Deel will surface)
- **Bank account details** for payouts (account number, routing/SWIFT, bank address). If you are using a Wise or Payoneer account, have those credentials ready instead.
- **A signed version of your Crest contract** (Bryant sends this separately; Deel will not move forward without it)

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Accept the Deel invitation">
    Click the link in the Deel invitation email. Set a strong password. Enable two-factor authentication immediately. Use your `@creststaff.com` email if one was issued, otherwise use the email Bryant invited.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your contract">
    Deel will display the Crest contract that Bryant prepared. Read it. Sign it inside Deel. If anything in the contract is wrong (rate, role, start date, jurisdiction), do not sign — DM Bryant first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete personal details">
    Legal name (must match the ID you will upload), date of birth, address, phone. Use your legal name as it appears on government documents, not a preferred or shortened version.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload identity documents">
    Photo ID and proof of address. Use clear photographs or scans; Deel's vendor will reject blurry images.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete your tax form">
    Deel auto-selects the right form based on your residency. US-based: W-9. Non-US: W-8BEN or local equivalent. If you are unsure of your tax classification (sole proprietor, single-member LLC, corporation), ask Bryant before submitting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add banking details">
    Local bank account, Wise, or Payoneer. Add at least one payout method. Verify the account holder name matches your legal name on the profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize the background check">
    Deel will surface the background check authorization step once the rest of the profile is complete. Click through, authorize, and watch for a separate vendor email asking for any clarifications.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common pitfalls

- **Name mismatch.** Legal name on Deel must match the ID. "Michael T. Smith" on your passport but "Mike Smith" on your profile triggers a manual review.
- **Expired proof of address.** Anything older than 90 days will be rejected. Online bank PDFs are easiest.
- **Wrong tax form.** Picking the US W-9 when you are not a US tax resident creates a mess that Deel can take a week to unwind.
- **No two-factor authentication.** Crest requires 2FA on every account that touches payment information. Set it up before you log out.
- **Forgetting to authorize the background check.** Profile completion alone does not trigger the check. You must click the explicit authorization.
